# Content Marketing

Content marketing is the practice of building an audience by consistently creating content that is genuinely useful to your target customer — before they become a customer. Done well, it makes your brand the trusted authority in your category and fills your pipeline with buyers who already believe in you before the first sales call.

Done badly, it is a blog nobody reads.

---

## Content Strategy Foundation

Before writing a word of content, answer four questions:

**1. Who is this for?**
Specific personas, not generic job titles. "Head of Finance at a 50–200 person SA tech company, responsible for SARS compliance, fighting for budget from the CEO, reads Business Day and Moneyweb" is a persona. "CFOs" is not.

**2. What do they actually need?**
Map content to the buyer journey:

| Stage | Mental state | Content type |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Unaware | "I have a problem" | Educational: how-to guides, industry data, explainers |
| Aware | "Solutions exist" | Comparative: frameworks, category overviews, case studies |
| Considering | "Is this right for me?" | Proof: ROI calculators, detailed case studies, demos |
| Decision | "Should I do this now?" | Conversion: trials, proposals, guarantees |

**3. What are we uniquely positioned to say?**
Your content must have a point of view that only your brand can credibly hold. Content that could be published by any company in your industry adds no brand equity.

**4. How will it get discovered?**
Content without a distribution plan is a diary. Build distribution into the content strategy before you start producing.

## The Editorial Calendar

An editorial calendar is the content strategy made executable.

**Planning cadence:**
- **Quarterly**: Content themes aligned to business priorities; campaign calendar; major asset planning
- **Monthly**: Specific topics, authors, deadlines, distribution channels
- **Weekly**: Production status, social scheduling, performance review

**Minimum viable calendar fields:**
- Topic and headline
- Target persona and buyer stage
- Primary keyword (if SEO-driven)
- Author and deadline
- Channels (blog, LinkedIn, email, YouTube, etc.)
- Status (brief / draft / review / scheduled / live)
- Performance target (views / leads / shares)

## Content Types and When to Use Them

### Long-form (1,500–3,000 words)
- **Best for**: SEO, thought leadership, building authority
- **Resource intensive**: Yes — invest in long-form only when you have keyword research or a clear distribution strategy
- **SA context**: Business Day, Mail & Guardian, and Moneyweb accept contributed articles; a well-placed byline is worth 10 blog posts

### Short-form (300–800 words)
- **Best for**: Social content (especially LinkedIn), newsletter content, quick guides
- **Cadence**: Can sustain 2–4 per week if well-planned

### Video
- **YouTube**: Evergreen how-to content; strong SA search volume on technical topics
- **LinkedIn video**: 60–90 second thought leadership clips; outperforms text posts in reach
- **Explainer / product**: Highest production cost; reuse across website, sales, and campaigns

### Webinars and events
- **SA context**: In-person events remain high-value in South Africa; decision-makers attend industry events that would be ignored as a webinar
- **Live webinars** work best with a known speaker or genuinely novel content (data releases, regulatory updates)

### Case studies
The highest-value content asset in B2B. A well-written case study:
- Names the customer (or gives enough detail to infer the sector)
- States the before state (the problem, the cost)
- Describes the solution
- Quantifies the result (R, %, time)
- Includes a customer quote

Most companies have the data for 10 compelling case studies and no program to produce them. This is always worth fixing.

### Data and original research
Original survey data or proprietary analysis creates content assets that competitors cannot replicate:
- Press coverage (free distribution)
- Social sharing (peers sharing with peers)
- Sales tool (data that validates the buyer's problem)
- Annual reports create evergreen brand equity ("State of [Industry] in SA 2025")

## Thought Leadership

A thought leader is someone whose views change how others think about a problem — not someone who publishes frequently.

**Building a thought leader:**
1. Choose one or two clear topics where the leader can hold a genuine opinion
2. Develop a contrarian but defensible point of view (not bland industry consensus)
3. Produce consistently: monthly long-form + weekly LinkedIn activity is the minimum viable program
4. Pitch media for speaking slots and contributed articles
5. Measure: engagement rate on posts, speaking invitations, inbound enquiries citing their content

**SA-specific platforms for thought leadership:**
- LinkedIn (most effective for B2B SA audiences)
- Moneyweb Contributed Articles
- BizCommunity (marketing professionals)
- Business Day Op-ed / Sunday Times Business
- AfricaCom, Africa Tech Week, ITWeb summits (speaking)

## Content Operations

**Content production workflow:**
1. Brief (topic, keyword, persona, angle, CTA, word count)
2. Outline review
3. First draft
4. Editorial review (accuracy, brand voice, messaging alignment)
5. Legal/compliance review (if required — financial services, healthcare)
6. SEO optimisation
7. Design / visual assets
8. Scheduling and publication
9. Distribution (email, social, sales team)
10. Performance tracking (at 7, 30, 90 days)

**Content governance:**
- Define brand voice: 3–5 adjectives that describe HOW the brand sounds (e.g., direct, warm, expert, never corporate)
- Brand voice guide with examples of "we say this / we don't say this"
- Style guide: terminology, capitalisation, preferred phrasings

## Content Performance Metrics

| Metric | What it tells you |
|--------|------------------|
| Organic traffic | Content is being discovered via search |
| Time on page | Content is genuinely engaging (target >2 mins) |
| Scroll depth | How far readers get (below 50% = problem) |
| Leads generated | Content converts interest to pipeline |
| Social shares and engagement | Content resonates enough to share |
| Email open and CTR | Newsletter content quality |
| Content-influenced pipeline | Revenue where content was a touchpoint |
