# Social Media Strategy

Social media is simultaneously the most misunderstood and most under-leveraged channel in B2B marketing. Most companies treat it as a broadcast channel — pushing announcements and product content. The brands that win treat it as a conversation channel — building relationships, demonstrating expertise, and earning trust with the people who will eventually buy from them.

---

## SA Social Media Landscape

Understanding who is on which platform in South Africa is the foundation of platform selection:

| Platform | SA active users (est.) | Key demographics | B2B value |
|----------|----------------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Facebook | 18–20 million | Broad; 25–54 strongest; all income groups | Medium — good for community and events |
| WhatsApp | 24+ million | Near-universal; dominant messaging app | High — private channel; business accounts growing |
| YouTube | 14–16 million | Broad; strong 18–34; growing rural | High — second search engine; long-form content |
| Instagram | 5–7 million | Urban 18–35; lifestyle and consumer brands | Low-Medium for B2B |
| LinkedIn | 6–8 million | Professionals, decision-makers, graduates | Very High for B2B |
| TikTok | 5–7 million | Under 30; growing; entertainment-first | Low for B2B currently |
| X/Twitter | 2–3 million | Tech, media, politics; declining | Low; good for news monitoring |

**The WhatsApp reality**: WhatsApp is where South Africans actually communicate. B2B referrals, customer service, informal sales conversations — all happen on WhatsApp. WhatsApp Business API (for larger organisations) enables structured customer comms, while WhatsApp Groups are a critical community-building tool.

## Platform Strategy by Business Type

### B2B Technology / Professional Services
**Primary**: LinkedIn (organic + paid)
**Secondary**: YouTube (thought leadership video), X/Twitter (monitoring and occasional engagement)
**Community**: WhatsApp Business for customer groups
**Avoid**: Spreading thin across all platforms with no clear strategy

### B2C Consumer / Retail
**Primary**: Facebook + Instagram (connected via Meta Business Suite)
**Secondary**: TikTok (growing; essential for under-30 audiences)
**Community**: Facebook Groups, WhatsApp newsletters
**Commerce**: Instagram Shopping, Facebook Shops for ecommerce

### SMB / Local Services
**Primary**: Facebook (highest SA reach, local business tools)
**Secondary**: Google Business Profile (reviews, local search) — technically not social but managed alongside social
**Community**: WhatsApp Business

## LinkedIn Organic Strategy (B2B Priority)

LinkedIn is the highest-value social platform for B2B in South Africa. An effective LinkedIn presence:

### Company page
- Consistent posting: minimum 3× per week
- Content mix: 40% thought leadership, 30% company news / culture, 20% product / service, 10% engagement (polls, questions)
- Employee advocacy: company posts get 2–5× more reach when employees engage with them in the first hour of posting
- Native LinkedIn articles outperform external links in feed distribution

### Founder / executive personal brand
Personal profiles almost always outreach company pages by 10–20×. The CEO, CMO, or subject matter expert with an active personal brand is the brand's most effective social asset.
- Post frequency: minimum 3–5× per week to grow; 1–2× per week to maintain
- Hook in first line: LinkedIn cuts off at "...see more" — first 1–2 sentences must earn the click
- Content types that perform on SA LinkedIn: contrarian opinions, behind-the-scenes, data/research, carousels, short personal stories with a business lesson
- Engage with comments: comments within the first 60 minutes dramatically increase post reach

---

## Content Formats and Performance

**LinkedIn**:
- Text posts with strong hooks perform consistently
- Carousels (image posts with swipe-through slides) have high engagement
- Native video (uploaded directly, not YouTube links) gets 3–5× more reach than external links
- Polls generate high engagement but low depth

**YouTube**:
- Thumbnails are more important than titles for click-through rate
- Optimal length for B2B: 8–15 minutes (long enough to be substantive; short enough to watch in one session)
- Chapters and timestamps improve retention and search indexing
- Always add captions (SA audience diversity; many users watch without sound)

**Instagram**:
- Reels get significantly higher reach than static posts
- Stories for daily touchpoints; highlights for evergreen brand content
- Carousels have high save rates (a signal Instagram weights positively)

## Influencer Marketing

In South Africa, influencer marketing is most effective for B2C consumer brands. B2B influencer marketing (sometimes called "expert marketing") works differently.

### B2C influencer tiers in SA

| Tier | Followers | CPP (cost per post) | Best use |
|------|-----------|---------------------|---------|
| Mega (celebrity) | 500K+ | R30,000–R200,000+ | Mass awareness campaigns |
| Macro | 100K–500K | R8,000–R30,000 | Broad category reach |
| Mid-tier | 30K–100K | R2,000–R8,000 | Niche audience targeting |
| Micro | 5K–30K | R500–R2,000 | High engagement, authentic |
| Nano | 1K–5K | R0–R500 / gifting | Hyper-local, community trust |

**SA influencer market nuances:**
- Authenticity threshold is lower with SA audiences than US/UK — creators who are visibly paid but genuine still convert
- Diversity and representation: SA audiences respond negatively to campaigns that do not reflect SA's demographics
- FAN/FTC equivalent: the Advertising Regulatory Board (ARB) requires disclosure of paid partnerships (#ad, #sponsored, or "paid partnership" label)
- Micro-influencers (5K–30K) often deliver better ROI per rand than macro-influencers in SA due to lower costs and higher engagement rates

### B2B "expert marketing"
In B2B, the influencers are subject matter experts with LinkedIn audiences, podcast listeners, or conference followings — not traditional Instagram influencers. Partnering with:
- Industry analysts and consultants
- Media editors and journalists
- Conference speakers
- Respected practitioners with large personal brand followings

These partnerships work through guest content, co-authored research, speaking alongside them, or sponsoring their events.

## Community Management

A brand's social presence is not just content publishing — it is managing the conversations that happen around the brand.

**Response SLAs (standards)**:
- Direct messages: within 4 hours during business hours
- Public comments (positive): acknowledge within 24 hours
- Public complaints: respond within 2 hours; move to DM for resolution
- Reviews (Google, Hellopeter, social): respond to every review, positive or negative

**Hellopeter**: South Africa's dominant consumer review platform. B2C brands must actively monitor and respond — a pattern of unaddressed negative reviews is a major trust issue in SA.

**Crisis social media**:
1. Monitor proactively: Google Alerts, Mention, or Brandwatch for brand mentions
2. Pre-approved response templates for common complaint types
3. Escalation path: when to take offline, when to involve legal/PR
4. A social media crisis is any issue that is spreading faster than you can respond — mobilise immediately; delayed responses become the story

## Social Media KPIs

| KPI | What it measures | Target benchmark |
|-----|-----------------|-----------------|
| Reach | How many unique accounts see content | Growing MoM |
| Impressions | Total content views | 3–5× reach ratio normal |
| Engagement rate | Likes + comments + shares / reach | LinkedIn 2–5%; Instagram 1–4% |
| Follower growth | Audience building | 5–10% MoM early stage |
| Share of voice | Your mentions vs competitors | Growing against category |
| Leads from social | Pipeline attributed to social | Track via UTM + CRM |
| Community response rate | % of comments/messages responded to | >90% |
